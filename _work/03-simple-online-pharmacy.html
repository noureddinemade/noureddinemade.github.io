The existing NHS product had a variety of UX issues as well as not being ready for the business to start scaling the NHS side.

The web app had already had a lot of work done prior to my arrival so we wanted to focus on the app first, which had not been worked on since it was made.

The goal of this project was to create a better experience for the NHS product, as well as creating a patient area that could cater to all areas of the business and not just for the NHS.

However the MVP was focused on the main flows of the NHS product; sign up, add medication and request medication.

Research

There had already been a significant amount of research done prior to me and the timeframe to get the app designed was tight so I opted to not to focus a lot of the time on research.

I started by looking at the existing app, mapping out the core flows and designing basic wireframes.

The good thing about starting with the app was that we always wanted to take the mobile first approach to designing the web app as over 70% of our patients used a mobile to access the web app. This meant that apart from a few mobile only features and design specifics, we could use the majority of the components designed for mobile, for the web app.

Design

I wanted to keep the design as clean and minimal as possible. I wanted to ensure that the app didn’t appear to be designed for a specific audience. Our product had such a broad target audience of anyone who has repeat prescriptions, so the design had to cater to everyone.

Conclusion

After the app was designed and a lot of progress had been done on the front end development of the app, issues on the backend caused the project to stop. Due to a lot of different circumstances we couldn’t proceed with the development of the app because the old backend system was unable to handle many of the new requirements of the new system.

This meant that we would need to stop work on the app and start working on the admin area. A decision that we should have made from the beginning but unfortunately we were occupied with making a shiny new patient facing app.
