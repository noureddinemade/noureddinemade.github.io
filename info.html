in an ideal world, I’d be eating nuggets and relaxing by the pool. instead, I’m coming up with this dumb headline.

I’ve spent the last fifteen years doing design, so presumably, I know what I’m doing. And if that’s not enough and you’re still wondering if I know what design is, of course I do. It’s two single colour shapes on a white background surrounded with lots of white space.

In those fifteen years I’ve worked on a bunch of stuff including designing a website for the New Zealand All Blacks, working at a dogshit start up now called Pollen, teaching design at General Assembly, branding a huge digital pharmacy, AND coming up with this list.

Creative problem solving is at the heart of everything I do. Over the years, as titles changed and technology evolved, I figured out that my role is simple, I try and solve problems. So, being a designer, to me, means solving problems but in a creative way. Am I rambling yet? So as an example, if someone wanted their door to stop squeaking I would grease the hinges (obviously) but then I would also cover the door with glitter, because design.

Skills that pay the bills for my football shirt collection.

End-to-end product design, Interface design, User experience design, Webflow, Front-End development, Product strategy, Coming up with lists, Listening to the same song over and over until I’m sick of it, telling Jeff that his “Tinder meets LinkedIn” app idea is shit, and many other very useful skills.

My client list could fill an ocean, but that’s just how repeating things works.

General Assembly, CDM London, Universal Music, Woolworths, Rexona, MuslimAid Australia, David Jones, New Zealand All Blacks, Indomie New Zealand, Crabtree & Evelyn, New Republique, University of Western Sydney, NPS MedicineWise and Create Studios.

When I’m not designing, you can find me hanging out with my partner and our cat. Maybe we’re rewatching Lost and I’m getting mad at Sayid’s terrible Arabic. Maybe we’re watching football, and by we I mean me. Maybe I’m eating a cone shaped ice cream. The point is, I’m doing stuff and I don’t need to prove it.
