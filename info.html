in an ideal world, I’d be eating a bunch of nuggets while relaxing by the pool. instead, I’m here coming up with this dumb headline for my portfolio.

IMAGE

Ever since I got my first job as a designer, I’ve known that I was going to be a designer. It was one of those moments of clarity where I was like “I guess I’ll do this now”.

I’ve spent the last fifteen years doing design, so presumably, I know what I’m doing. And if that’s not enough and you’re still wondering if I know what design is, of course I do. It’s two black squares in the middle of a white sheet with a bunch of space around them.

In those fifteen years I’ve worked on a bunch of stuff including designing a website for the New Zealand All Blacks, working at a dogshit start up now called Pollen, teaching design at General Assembly, branding a huge digital pharmacy, AND coming up with this list.

When I’m not designing, you can find me hanging out with my partner and our cat. Maybe we’re rewatching Lost and I’m getting mad at Sayid’s terrible Arabic. Maybe we’re watching football, and by we I mean me. Maybe I’m eating a cone shaped ice cream. The point is, I’m doing stuff and I don’t need to prove it.
